# UI Experience Audit — Full Inventory

**Date:** 2026-07-16 · 107 confirmed findings (11 auditors + code fact-check; 9 duplicates removed, 0 evidence-wrong)

## Stage summaries

### landing

The landing page is compact, on-brand, and mechanically well-crafted (explicit transitions, real touch targets, text-balance/pretty, tabular-nums), and its one-action structure is right for a phone. But as a first public impression it under-persuades: the product is never shown — a visual app (synced, furigana-annotated, colour-paired lyrics) is pitched with three text cards whose copy drifts into insider jargon (SOV/SVO, readings "adopted"/"flagged"). Trust plumbing is incomplete for public launch: no Privacy/Terms links on the page, the strongest privacy claim is rendered at ~2.1:1 contrast, and shared links carry no meta description/OG tags. "Get started" is honest (it lands on a clear empty library with an Add button), though it immediately stacks a 3-step modal that half-repeats the pitch, and the landing never foreshadows the multi-minute on-device alignment that follows the first added song.

**Strengths (do not regress):**

- Interaction craft already meets the checklist: explicit transition properties (no transition:all), active:scale press states, min-h-11/min-h-12 targets with touch-manipulation, tabular-nums on badges, text-balance/text-pretty throughout (LandingScreen.tsx:35,57,74-76,90) — fixes must not regress this.
- Privacy-first positioning is genuine and differentiating: 'all on your device' (LandingScreen.tsx:51) and 'nothing is uploaded' (LandingScreen.tsx:97) — keep the claims, only fix their visibility.
- Layout is safe at 375px: single-column max-w-2xl px-5 stack, sm: breakpoints for the 3-card grid, no fixed widths or nowrap strings, h-[100dvh] overflow-y-auto root — nothing clips or overflows (LandingScreen.tsx:28-29,65).
- The landing gate never crashes and is skippable: localStorage reads/writes are try/catch-wrapped and a persistent header escape hatch ('Open the app') exists for returning users who cleared storage (App.tsx:22-36, LandingScreen.tsx:32-38).

### library

The library stage is closer to launch-ready than most first screens: the empty state is designed and honest, deletes are confirm-guarded with failure-path recovery copy, and card interactions are keyboard-accessible with generous hit areas. The launch risks cluster in three areas: (1) fixed overlays — the offline/update banners paint over the header and Settings button, and standalone-PWA safe-area insets are ignored at top and bottom despite viewport-fit=cover; (2) data freshness — the song list is fetched once on mount, so returning users see a false "Your library is empty" flash and deletions made inside Settings leave ghost cards that open dead songs; (3) transient feedback — instructional warning/error toasts vanish after a fixed 4 seconds, faster than a stranger can read them. Everything else is P2 polish (badge jargon, press feedback on the invisible overlay button, sub-44px targets in banners/toasts, low-contrast tertiary controls).

**Strengths (do not regress):**

- Empty library state is deliberate and guiding: icon tile, text-balance headline, text-pretty body naming the two real add paths ("YouTube link or an audio file"), sitting directly under a persistent full-width "＋ Add a song" CTA (LibraryScreen.tsx:53-72) — do not regress this when fixing loading states.
- Delete flow is trust-correct: red ✕ with aria-label, ConfirmDialog stating permanent consequences ("and its saved audio/lyrics will be permanently removed"), bottom-sheet placement on mobile (items-end sm:items-center), and a warning toast with a concrete recovery path when audio deletion fails (LibraryScreen.tsx:25-37,112-121; ConfirmDialog.tsx:21,33).
- Accessibility/motion discipline is above baseline: full-card overlay button with aria-label and focus-visible ring (LibraryScreen.tsx:81-86), min-h-11 targets on header/delete controls, explicit transition properties everywhere (no transition:all), tabular-nums on the onboarding step counter (Onboarding.tsx:62), and global font-smoothing + neutral img outlines in index.css:5-24.
- Onboarding is short, skippable, and copy-accurate: its three steps match the actual add-song capabilities, with animated progress dots and a "1 of 3" counter (Onboarding.tsx:5-9,51-64).

### addsong

The add-song stage is structurally strong — a two-path chooser with honest trade-offs, auto-prefilled metadata with provenance badges, and a fuzzy-match confirmation gate — and a stranger can usually get from file/link to a saved song. But several feedback behaviors read as broken or lossy on a phone: the lyric-search panel shows a progress bar pinned at 0% with a "1/1" counter for a search that can run 1–2 minutes; an accidental backdrop tap silently discards pasted lyrics and typed metadata; a failed search dead-ends with no "search again" path; and internal jargon (LRCLIB, synced/plain) leaks into nearly every status string. Craft is mid-tier: the core manual-lyrics controls are ~28px tall, and key helper copy is 10px at 25% white opacity on near-black. No hard P0 stalls, but the cluster of P1s would erode a first-time visitor's trust at exactly the moment the app asks them to invest effort.

**Strengths (do not regress):**

- Source-choice tiles with an honest Includes/Limitations comparison and a Recommended badge (AddSongSheet.tsx:23-55) — a stranger understands the upload-vs-YouTube trade-off before committing; do not flatten this into a plain toggle.
- Metadata provenance UX: 'From file tags'/'From filename' badges, the ambiguous-filename amber warning, and one-tap 'Swap title and artist' (UploadAudioFlow.tsx:55-62, 334-348) — quietly excellent recovery affordances.
- Fuzzy-match confirmation gate: queried song vs matched entry comparison, 3-line preview, and an explicit 'Yes, this is the right song' before Add enables (LyricsFoundConfirm.tsx:31-103) — prevents the worst outcome (wrong lyrics aligned to a song).
- Specific, actionable YouTube error copy (youtube.ts:78-95: private/embedding-disabled vs 404 vs network) and the busy-gated close confirmation (useConfirmedClose.ts + ConfirmDialog.tsx) — keep both, extend rather than replace.

### alignwait

The auto-align wait is structurally strong for a multi-minute on-device job: staged step labels with honest details, a dual Overall/Current-task progress layout, first-run download messaging with size hints and substeps, a guarded cancel, and an honest low-confidence "done" state. The gaps are at the edges a stranger actually hits: the ~240MB model download auto-starts with no consent gate on the first song, browser navigation during the run loses everything silently, the crash-downgrade retry (a genuinely great mechanism) is wired to the wrong stage so the user just sees the bar snap back to 0, and unclassified errors surface raw exception text. Copy has several launch-blocking leaks — a "see docs/DEPLOYMENT.md" string, and post-run guidance that names controls ("tap-sync", "Vocal separation") that don't exist under those names in the UI. Craft is close: the opt-in rows have an invisible surface (900/80 on 900), and the only escape control during the wait is a 3.7:1-contrast caption-styled button.

**Strengths (do not regress):**

- Cancel during processing is properly guarded: ConfirmDialog with honest copy ('Speech recognition and alignment are still running. Stopping now discards all progress.'), destructive 'Stop' vs safe 'Keep running' (src/ai-pipeline/AutoAlignFlow.tsx:503-517, src/core/ui/ConfirmDialog.tsx) — do not weaken this when touching cancel paths.
- First-run download honesty is excellent: 'First run — downloading speech model (~240MB)', per-file percent ('Downloading model files (43%) — file'), aggregate-preferred progress, download→init substep checklist, and 'can take several minutes on first load' (AutoAlignFlow.tsx:56-91, 457-467). The ~1.5GB hint correctly swaps in when high-accuracy is selected (line 127).
- The runtime fallback ladder (word→segment, medium→small on WASM/OOM crashes, AutoAlignFlow.tsx:260-296) rescues multi-minute runs instead of failing them — any fix to its visibility (finding below) must preserve the behavior itself.
- ProcessProgress/ProgressBar craft fundamentals are right: tabular-nums on every counter (ProcessProgress.tsx:77,94,103), transition-[width] not transition:all (ProgressBar.tsx:32), role=status + aria-live=polite + descriptive aria-label, split enter animation, text-pretty on status lines. The done state honestly reports weak alignments in yellow instead of faking success (AutoAlignFlow.tsx:611-620).

### play

Play mode's information design is genuinely strong — current-line emphasis, staged A-B loop guidance, honest progress strips — but at 375px the screen budget and layering break down. Fixed offline/update banners overlay the header (Back + Play/Edit toggle), up to three in-flow strips can stack above the lyrics, and the fully-collapsed controls dock still consumes ~42% of a 667px-tall viewport, leaving roughly three visible lyric lines on the daily-use screen. The tap-word popover — the flagship learning interaction — dismisses via outside-tap that also triggers whatever is underneath (lyric taps seek playback) and its bottom-card position collides with the dock. Emoji-glyph icons (⏮ ▶ ⏸ 🔊 ⏩) render as color emoji on iOS and sub-44px targets pervade the compact dock, undercutting craft on the exact device the bar targets. No P0 stalls: a stranger can play a song, follow along, and find Display/Loop/Speed controls.

**Strengths (do not regress):**

- Current-line emphasis is excellent and must be preserved: 16px→20px size jump + weight + white vs white/45 + cinnabar glow (textShadow '0 0 20px rgba(248,113,113,0.5)') + inset ring (lyricRowPlayheadActive), all on scoped 300ms transitions (LyricDisplay.tsx:14-17,128,138; toolbarClasses.ts:56-57).
- A-B loop guidance is a model of stateful next-step copy: pulsing 'Now tap a lyric line to place point A' while arming, then 'Point A is set. Tap Set B…' idle hints, plus a role=alert error line (PlayerControls.tsx:280-303).
- Empty-lyrics state does everything right: icon, plain-language status, and a concrete next step ('Switch to Edit mode to add or import lyrics') (LyricDisplay.tsx:346-354).
- Interaction fundamentals are consistently applied: no transition:all anywhere (explicit transition-[color,background-color,…] lists), tabular-nums on every timer/loop chip, active:scale-[0.96] press states, and safe-area-inset padding on the dock (toolbarClasses.ts:33-47; PlayerControls.tsx:1351).

### edit

Edit mode's core interaction design is unusually safety-conscious — arm/confirm delete with auto-disarm, an undo/redo stack, a destructive-action confirm before Auto-align, and a prioritized single-hint system whose copy is mostly human ('these lyrics may not match this recording' is genuinely good). But at the 375px public-launch bar it falls short in four ways: the toolbar wraps into ragged multi-row chrome that eats a third of the viewport with no action hierarchy; the timestamp popover physically cannot reach the badly-off lines its own hints send users to fix (±15s fixed window, 1px-per-0.1s slider precision, unused playhead prop, no nudge buttons); the AlignmentEditor sub-screen fails the phone bar outright (full-screen with no cancel, originals truncated unreadable, ~24px controls beside an instant clear); and the undo stack silently reverts external changes like gap recovery. Guidance typography (everything at 10px) and emoji-as-icons undercut an otherwise disciplined visual system.

**Strengths (do not regress):**

- Destructive-action safety on delete is exemplary: two-tap arm ('Confirm?') with a 3s auto-disarm timer (EditMode.tsx:68, 317-328, 184-188) plus a 50-deep undo stack — do not regress this while restyling rows.
- The shared toolbarClasses system (src/core/ui/toolbarClasses.ts) is disciplined craft: min-h-11 targets, scoped transition-[color,border-color,transform] (never transition:all), active:scale-[0.96], tabular-nums on timestamps — any toolbar restructure must keep using these tokens.
- The prioritized hint stack shows exactly one most-actionable hint (EditMode.tsx:363-393) with its remedy button inline ('Re-align accurately') instead of dumping every warning at once — preserve the single-hint discipline when editing copy.
- Timestamp popover scrubbing live-previews the audio and reverts the playhead on cancel (previewReturnRef, EditMode.tsx:284-304; drafts committed only on Done) — a genuinely respectful preview/commit model to build the nudge buttons on top of.

### translation

The translation-attach pipeline is architecturally strong — smart attach with a confirm preview, an LRC timeline merge, pre-filled lyric-site search links, and undo-safe apply — but its edges fail the phone-stranger bar. The happy path (paste → confirm → looks good) reads well; the unhappy path is where strangers land and stall: a mismatched line count drops them into a fullscreen manual editor with no cancel, no explanation, truncated unreadable originals at 375px, sub-24px controls, and silently discarded surplus lines. Discoverability is one-sided: Play mode hides every trace of the translation feature until a translation already exists, and the feature wears three different names across the Edit toolbar, modal, and Display menu. Bilingual rendering itself (stacked hierarchy, sub-420px side-by-side fallback, EditMode translation rows) is in good shape and mostly needs guardrail copy rather than redesign.

**Strengths (do not regress):**

- Smart-attach confirm step (SecondLanguagePanel.tsx:183-208) is honest UX: preview pairs, 'Looks good' / 'Fix pairings' / re-paste escape hatches, and apply routes through EditMode's applyChange so attaching a translation is fully undoable (EditMode.tsx:574).
- FindLyricsOnlineSection (SecondLanguagePanel.tsx:26-64 + lyricSiteLinks.ts) is a genuine stranger accelerator: direction-aware pre-filled search links (Animelyrics/LyricsTranslate vs UtaTen/Uta-Net/LRCLIB) with a graceful no-metadata fallback line — keep it in both the current and paste phases.
- Bilingual Play-mode hierarchy holds at 375px: active JP line text-xl + italic white/70 translation, de-emphasized inactive lines, side-by-side auto-collapsing to one column below 420px (LyricDisplay.tsx:252-299), and token↔translation-word hover pairing — none of this should be disturbed by attach-flow fixes.
- Timed-LRC awareness: a synced secondary LRC paste merges by timestamp ('timeline' method, lineAligner.ts:920-926) and the paste hint explicitly invites 'an LRC block', so power users skip pairing entirely.

### cloze

Stage 7 does not exist in the shipped UI. ClozeOverlay is exported but never imported anywhere in src/, setClozeMode is never called, clozeMode is never read, clozeDifficulty has no settings control, and the PracticeStats cloze counters are never written — git history (commit 1983b07, June 15) shows the engine/overlay/test were added but the mount never happened. A public-launch stranger cannot discover practice mode because there is no entry point, toggle, or mention of it in any screen. Judged as-designed, the component also lacks the entire interaction loop the stage requires: no answer input, no right/wrong feedback, no session progress or exit, and the blanking itself is defeatable via long-press select/copy/screen-reader since the answer text is merely rendered transparent. The launch decision is binary: either wire a minimal practice loop into the player (large effort) or delete src/cloze plus the orphaned store fields (small effort) so the codebase stops implying a shipped feature.

**Strengths (do not regress):**

- POS-based blank selection is pedagogically right for Japanese (ClozeEngine.ts:7-8): particles and function words stay visible as scaffolding while content words are hidden — keep this core logic when wiring the feature.
- ClozeOverlay reuses the app's established lyric typography and theme (ClozeOverlay.tsx:20,28: lang="ja", font-jp, text-xl sm:text-2xl font-semibold, cinnabar accent for revealed answers), so a wired version would match the play view rather than look bolted-on; flex-wrap at token boundaries also gives correct Japanese line-breaking on 375px.
- Difficulty preference is already persisted (LyricsStore.ts:57 partialize, SettingsStore.ts:21), so once a UI exists a returning user's choice survives reload with no extra work.
- The engine preserves full Token data on each cloze token ({...token, blanked}, ClozeEngine.ts:21), so furigana and tap-lookup can be reattached to revealed tokens without re-tokenizing.

### settings

Settings is one of the healthier screens: grouping is sane (language → behavior toggles → storage → library → support → legal), the recently compacted switch rows are accessible and well-built, song deletion is properly two-step with honest failure toasts, and the storage card gives a truthful breakdown with an actionable near-full warning. No P0s. What keeps it below the public-launch bar on a phone: the AI-facing copy leaks pipeline jargon ("Auto-align vocal separation", "Demucs", "high-confidence sung readings", bare "LRC" button); the two storage maintenance links are near-invisible (white/30, ~2.6:1) sub-44px targets, and "Clear AI model cache" wipes a multi-hundred-MB download in one unconfirmed tap; and the sheet itself has three mobile ergonomics gaps — the close button scrolls away inside the 90dvh sheet, the last card sits under the iOS home indicator (viewport-fit=cover with only 16px bottom padding), and the sheet appears with no enter motion. All fixes are S-effort copy/class changes; none touch the recently shipped switch-row or mode-switch work.

**Strengths (do not regress):**

- Destructive-safe song deletion: inline two-step 'Delete forever?' confirm (SettingsView.tsx:254-272), error toast on failure, and a graceful orphaned-audio fallback toast pointing at the cleanup tool (line 104) — do not regress this while restyling the row.
- Switch rows are genuinely well-engineered: role="switch" + aria-checked + aria-label, a 44px hit area (min-h-11 min-w-11) wrapping the compact visual track, and scoped transitions (transition-colors, transition-[left]) — no transition:all anywhere in the file (SettingsView.tsx:44-65).
- Storage card honesty: real per-bucket breakdown with tabular-nums values (lines 198-213), an >80% red warning with actionable copy ('Delete songs to free space', line 215), and proactive orphaned-file detection with counts (lines 217-221).
- Sheet accessibility fundamentals: the backdrop is a real <button aria-label="Close"> (SettingsSheet.tsx:13), and the layout responsively switches bottom-sheet → centered desktop card (justify-end md:justify-center, rounded-t-2xl md:rounded-2xl).

### cohesion

Token discipline is unusually good for an indie PWA: a shared toolbarClasses.ts bakes 44px heights, scoped transition properties, active:scale-[0.96] and tabular-nums into every chip; motion is a consistent 150ms/ease-out system with zero transition:all; index.css has font-smoothing and neutral image outlines. The drift that remains is concentrated in four places: two invalid opacity suffixes (bg-white/8, bg-cinnabar-950/98) that Tailwind silently drops — leaving the Play/Edit segmented control with no track; a mobile 'compact' tier that inverts touch ergonomics (phone gets 36px chips, desktop gets 44px); a 65-use text-[10px] tier that has leaked from micro-labels into multi-sentence guidance copy; and a danger color (red-400) that is byte-identical to the brand accent (#f87171), so inline errors don't read as errors. Secondary drift — a 14-step white/NN text ladder, amber-vs-yellow warning split, five different modal surface recipes — is P2 polish that accumulates into slight amateurishness but won't stall a stranger.

**Strengths (do not regress):**

- Shared token module src/core/ui/toolbarClasses.ts: toolbarActionBtn/toolbarChipBtn/timestampPillBtn all carry min-h-11, border-cinnabar-800, rounded-lg, scoped transition-[color,border-color,transform] duration-150 ease-out, active:scale-[0.96], tabular-nums — the Play/Edit toolbars, A-B chips and speed presets stay visually aligned because of it. Do not fork these when fixing findings.
- Motion system is disciplined: zero transition-all occurrences in src/; 42 of 52 durations are duration-150 with 45 ease-out; entrances share one progress-enter keyframe (tailwind.config.ts:23, reused at LandingScreen.tsx:42, ProcessProgress.tsx:60, Toast.tsx:34, Onboarding.tsx:50); progress-bar widths use scoped transition-[width].
- Live numbers are honest and stable: tabular-nums on 26 sites covering seek times (PlayerControls.tsx:1358), A-B loop chips (toolbarChipBtn), timestamps (EditMode.tsx:151), and the ProcessProgress component (aria-live, elapsed-seconds fallback, step counts) is reused for every long wait.
- Base hygiene per the craft checklist already ships: index.css has -webkit-font-smoothing:antialiased (line 7) and neutral white/black-alpha img outlines (lines 15-24); text inputs share one focus recipe (outline-none + focus:border-cinnabar-accent, e.g. UploadAudioFlow.tsx:263, LinkParser.tsx:250).

### copy

Copy is strong where it coaches (A-B loop hints, confirm dialogs, long-wait align progress) but leaks internal vocabulary at exactly the surfaces a stranger meets first: the add-song flow says "LRCLIB" in eight-plus instructional strings, the align flow surfaces "Demucs", "segment timestamps", and even a repo doc path, and status lines say "Tokenizing" and "embedding model". The bigger systemic problem is feature-name drift: the same re-align option is called "Accurate readings", "Word-level timestamps (slower)", and "Re-align accurately" on three surfaces; tap timing is "tap-sync" in one flow and "Tap-through" in another; and copy references control labels that don't exist ("Clean up orphaned audio", "Vocal separation"), so cross-references a stranger tries to follow break. Tone is otherwise consistent (sentence case, no emoji/exclamation), with minor US/UK spelling and brand-name (Utasync vs 歌sync) inconsistencies. Nothing hard-stalls a stranger (no P0), but the naming drift plus jargon would read as unfinished at a public launch; almost everything here is sub-hour string work.

**Strengths (do not regress):**

- A-B loop stateful coaching copy is excellent — PlayerControls.tsx:286-299 walks each state ('Tap Set A or Set B, then tap a lyric line to mark the loop.', 'Point A is set. Tap Set B, then tap the ending lyric line.'). Any loop-UI edits must preserve these stepwise hints.
- Error messages mostly say what to DO next: youtube.ts:85-94 ('This video is private or has embedding disabled. Try a different link, or add audio + lyrics manually.'), AutoAlignFlow.tsx:44-52 OOM/network variants ('try closing other tabs and retrying'), ErrorBoundary.tsx:35 ('Your saved songs are safe — reloading usually fixes it.').
- Long-wait honesty in the align pipeline is a trust asset: AutoAlignFlow.tsx:439-448 sets expectations ('can take a few minutes on phones', 'First run — downloading speech model (~240MB)', 'Packaging transcript — almost ready') and ProcessProgress shows elapsed seconds when percent is unknown. Don't flatten these into generic spinners.
- Destructive confirmations name stakes and use specific verb labels: 'Cancel auto-align?' → Stop/Keep running (AutoAlignFlow.tsx:504-516), 'Discard this song?' → Discard/Keep working (AddSongSheet.tsx:134-141), 'Delete forever?' (SettingsView.tsx:256), LibraryScreen delete dialog states audio+lyrics are permanently removed.
